## LECTURE 1: Introduction to Finance & Investing

### Core Concepts:
- **What is finance?** - The study of money management and investment
- **Time value of money** - A dollar today is worth more than a dollar tomorrow
- **Risk vs. Return** - Higher potential returns = higher risk (usually)
- **Compound interest** - The 8th wonder of the world (Einstein)

### Key Formulas:
```
Future Value = Present Value × (1 + r)^n
Present Value = Future Value / (1 + r)^n

Where:
r = interest rate / return rate
n = number of periods
```

### Shkreli's Take:
- Most people don't understand basic finance
- This ignorance is exploited by Wall Street
- Understanding these basics = unfair advantage

## LECTURE 2: Financial Statements

### The Three Core Statements:

**1. Income Statement (P&L)**
- Revenue (top line)
- Costs / Expenses
- Net Income (bottom line)
- Shows: How much money did we MAKE?

**2. Balance Sheet**
- Assets = Liabilities + Equity
- Shows: What do we OWN vs OWE?
- Snapshot in time

**3. Cash Flow Statement**
- Operating cash flow
- Investing cash flow
- Financing cash flow
- Shows: Where did the cash actually GO?

### Key Insight:
> "Net income is an opinion. Cash flow is a fact."

Companies can manipulate earnings, but cash is cash.

## LECTURE 3: Accounting Basics

### Important Concepts:
- **Accrual vs. Cash accounting** - When do you recognize revenue?
- **Depreciation** - Spreading asset costs over time
- **GAAP vs. Non-GAAP** - Official rules vs. "adjusted" numbers
- **Revenue recognition** - When can you count a sale?

### Red Flags to Watch:
- Growing revenue but shrinking cash flow
- Aggressive revenue recognition
- Unusual "one-time" charges every quarter
- Related party transactions

### Shkreli's Rule:
> "If you don't understand the accounting, you don't understand the company."

## LECTURE 4: Valuation Methods

### The Big Three:

**1. Discounted Cash Flow (DCF)**
- Project future cash flows
- Discount back to present value
- Most "correct" but requires many assumptions

```
Value = Σ (Cash Flow_t / (1 + r)^t) + Terminal Value
```

**2. Comparable Company Analysis ("Comps")**
- Find similar companies
- Compare valuation multiples (P/E, EV/EBITDA, etc.)
- "What are peers trading at?"

**3. Precedent Transactions**
- What did acquirers pay for similar companies?
- Includes control premium
- Historical M&A data

### Key Multiples:
- **P/E Ratio** = Price / Earnings per Share
- **EV/EBITDA** = Enterprise Value / EBITDA
- **P/S Ratio** = Price / Sales (for unprofitable companies)
- **P/B Ratio** = Price / Book Value

## LECTURE 5: Investment Analysis

### Building an Investment Thesis:

1. **Understand the business**
   - What do they actually do?
   - How do they make money?
   - What's the competitive advantage (moat)?

2. **Analyze the financials**
   - Revenue growth trends
   - Margin expansion/compression
   - Cash generation
   - Debt levels

3. **Assess the valuation**
   - Is it cheap or expensive relative to:
     - Its own history
     - Peers
     - Growth rate

4. **Identify catalysts**
   - What will make the stock move?
   - Earnings? New product? M&A?

5. **Understand the risks**
   - What could go wrong?
   - How bad could it get?

### Shkreli's Framework:
> "I want to know: What's the upside? What's the downside? What's the probability of each?"

## LECTURE 6: Biotech/Pharma Valuation (Shkreli's Specialty)

### Why Biotech is Different:
- Binary outcomes (drug works or doesn't)
- Long development timelines (10+ years)
- Regulatory risk (FDA approval)
- Patent cliffs

### Key Concepts:
- **Pipeline analysis** - What drugs are in development?
- **Clinical trial phases** - Phase 1, 2, 3 and what each means
- **Probability of success** - Historical success rates by phase
- **Peak sales estimates** - How big could this drug be?

### Biotech Valuation:
```
Value = Σ (Probability × NPV of each drug) + Cash - Burn
```

### Shkreli's Edge:
- Deep scientific understanding
- Reading clinical trial data
- Understanding FDA dynamics
- Knowing which management teams execute

## LECTURE 7: Risk Management

### Types of Risk:
- **Market risk** - Everything goes down together
- **Company-specific risk** - This company fucks up
- **Liquidity risk** - Can't sell when you need to
- **Concentration risk** - Too much in one position

### Position Sizing:
- Never bet so big that one loss kills you
- Size based on conviction AND risk
- Have a maximum position size rule

### Shkreli's Rules:
1. Know your downside before you enter
2. Have a thesis - know why you're in
3. Know when you're wrong - have an exit plan
4. Don't average down into a broken thesis

## LECTURE 8: Market Dynamics

### How Markets Actually Work:
- **Supply and demand** - Price is where buyers meet sellers
- **Market makers** - Provide liquidity, profit from spread
- **Institutional vs. retail** - Big money moves markets
- **Information flow** - Who knows what, when?

### Market Psychology:
- **Fear and greed** - The two main drivers
- **Momentum** - Winners keep winning (until they don't)
- **Mean reversion** - Extremes correct
- **Narrative** - Stories move stocks more than numbers

### Shkreli on Markets:
> "The market can stay irrational longer than you can stay solvent."

## LECTURE 9: Advanced Topics

### Short Selling:
- Borrow shares, sell them, buy back lower
- Unlimited downside (stock can go up forever)
- Need to pay borrow costs
- Risk of short squeeze

### Options:
- Calls (right to buy) and Puts (right to sell)
- Leverage - control more with less capital
- Greeks - Delta, Gamma, Theta, Vega
- Strategies - spreads, straddles, etc.

### Leverage:
- Amplifies gains AND losses
- Margin requirements
- Risk of forced liquidation
