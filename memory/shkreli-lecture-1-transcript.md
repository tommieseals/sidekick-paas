### The 6 Key Metrics (55:00 - 1:30:00)

**For EVERY stock, Shkreli looks at these 6 things:**

1. **Stock Price (P)** - Price per share
2. **Shares Outstanding (SO)** - Total number of shares
3. **Market Cap** = Stock Price × Shares Outstanding
4. **Cash** - Cash + Investments + Marketable Securities
5. **Debt** - Money owed to lenders
6. **Enterprise Value (EV)** = Market Cap - Cash + Debt

**Stock Price Doesn't Mean Much:**
- Chipotle: $500/share but ~$15B market cap
- Coca-Cola: $43/share but ~$185B market cap
- Chipotle stock is 10x the price but Coke is 10x bigger company

### Enterprise Value Explained (1:14:00 - 1:50:00)

**The Key Formula:**
```
Enterprise Value = Market Cap - Cash + Debt
```

**Why EV Matters:**

**Example 1 - Uncle Louie's Waste Management:**
- Buying for $10M (market cap)
- Makes $10M/year
- But wait... $30M debt and only $10M cash
- Net debt: $20M
- **Enterprise Value: $30M**
- Payback: 3 years, not 1 year!

**Example 2 - Cousin Vickram's Delis:**
- Buying for $5M (market cap)
- Makes $1M/year
- But... $3M cash, zero debt
- Net cash: +$3M
- **Enterprise Value: $2M**
- Payback: 2 years!
- Vickram got hustled

**The Box Analogy:**
- Market cap = price to buy the box
- Inside the box: cash (yours!) and debt (now yours too)
- EV = true cost of the business after adjusting for cash/debt

> "Enterprise Value is how much money it would take to buy the company."

### Balance Sheet Deep Dive (1:50:00 - 2:12:00)

**The Holy Trinity of Financial Statements:**
1. Income Statement (P&L)
2. Balance Sheet
3. Cash Flow Statement

**Balance Sheet = Snapshot in Time (as of specific date)**

**Structure:**
```
ASSETS (what you own)
- Cash
- Accounts Receivable (cash owed TO you)
- Inventory
- Property, Plant & Equipment
- Investments
= Total Assets

LIABILITIES (what you owe)
- Accounts Payable (cash you owe others)
- Debt
- Other obligations
= Total Liabilities

SHAREHOLDERS EQUITY = Assets - Liabilities
(Also called "Book Value")
```

**The Balance Sheet MUST Balance:**
```
Assets = Liabilities + Shareholders Equity
```

**Leverage:**
- Debt vs Cash ratio
- Too much leverage = dangerous
- Japan 1980s: Companies borrowed too much, crashed
- Now Japanese companies refuse debt (conservative)
- Sweet spot: Some debt is good, too much is dangerous
