### The Holy Trinity of Financial Statements (5:30 - 6:30)

**IS, BS, CF:**
1. **Income Statement (IS)** - How much money did we MAKE?
2. **Balance Sheet (BS)** - What do we OWN and OWE?
3. **Cash Flow Statement (CF)** - Where did cash actually GO?

> "You need all three to really interpret a company's performance. All three tell you slightly different information."

**For beginners:** Focus on Income Statement + Balance Sheet first. Cash Flow is more advanced.

## BALANCE SHEET DEEP DIVE (8:00 - 30:00)

### The Balance Sheet = Statement of Balances

**Listed in ORDER OF LIQUIDITY** (most liquid first)

**Liquidity = How fast can you convert it to cash**

### ASSETS (What You Own)

#### 1. Cash & Cash Equivalents (MOST LIQUID)
- Actual cash in bank accounts
- Example: Apple had $16B cash

#### 2. Marketable Securities
- Treasury bonds, stocks, etc.
- Almost cash but not quite
- Can sell quickly
- Example: Apple had $21B → Total cash-like: $38B

#### 3. Accounts Receivable (A/R)
- Money customers OWE you
- Typically collected in 30-60 days ("Net 30")
- Listed NET of bad debt
- Example: Apple had $13B in A/R

**Bad Debt:**
- Customers who won't pay
- Anything 60-90+ days past due = start writing off
- You estimate what won't be collected

#### 4. Inventories
Three types:
- **Raw Materials** - Glass, chips, etc.
- **Work in Progress (WIP)** - Half-finished products
- **Finished Goods** - Ready to sell

#### 5. Property, Plant & Equipment (PP&E)
**Critical concept - MEMORIZE THIS**

- Any hard asset with useful life > 12 months
- Land, buildings, factories, machines
- Even software with multi-year license
- Subject to DEPRECIATION (spreading cost over useful life)

> "PP&E turns out to be extremely vexing in accounting and quite important."

#### 6. Intangible Assets
- Goodwill (from acquisitions)
- Patents, trademarks, brand value
- Intellectual property
- Never directly convert to cash

### CURRENT vs NON-CURRENT ASSETS

**Current Assets:** Convertible to cash within 1 year
**Non-Current Assets:** Takes more than 1 year

## BALANCE SHEET EQUATION (Must Memorize)

```
ASSETS = LIABILITIES + SHAREHOLDERS EQUITY
```

Or rearranged:
```
SHAREHOLDERS EQUITY = ASSETS - LIABILITIES
```

**The balance sheet MUST BALANCE** - that's why it's called a balance sheet!

### LIABILITIES (What You Owe)

#### Current Liabilities (Due within 1 year)
- **Accounts Payable (A/P)** - Money you owe suppliers
- Short-term debt
- Accrued expenses
- Deferred revenue

#### Long-Term Liabilities
- Long-term debt
- Pension obligations
- Other long-term obligations

### SHAREHOLDERS EQUITY (Book Value)

What's left after subtracting liabilities from assets.

## INCOME STATEMENT OVERVIEW (30:00 - 45:00)

**Income Statement = Period of Time** (not a snapshot)
- Covers a quarter or a year
- Shows revenue, costs, and profit

### Basic Structure:
```
Revenue (Sales/Turnover)
- Cost of Goods Sold (COGS)
= Gross Profit

- Operating Expenses
  - R&D
  - SG&A (Selling, General & Administrative)
  - Depreciation & Amortization
= Operating Income (EBIT)

- Interest Expense
= Pre-tax Income

- Taxes
= Net Income
```

## KEY CONCEPTS INTRODUCED

### 1. Net 30 / Net 60
Standard business payment terms - pay within 30 or 60 days after receiving goods.

### 2. Bad Debt Expense
Write-off for receivables you won't collect. Usually after 60-90 days overdue.

### 3. Useful Life
How long an asset provides value. Determines if it's PP&E (>12 months) or expense.

### 4. Depreciation (Preview)
Spreading the cost of PP&E over its useful life. HUGE topic for later.

### 5. Goodwill
Accounting concept from acquisitions - the premium paid over book value.

## PRACTICAL APPLICATIONS FOR US

### For Job Search:
1. **Research target companies** - Pull their 10-K, look at financial health
2. **Check cash position** - Are they flush or struggling?
3. **Look at revenue trends** - Growing or shrinking?
4. **Understand their business** through financial statements

### For Evaluating Opportunities:
1. **Accounts Receivable aging** - How fast do they collect?
2. **Inventory levels** - Too much = bad sign (can't sell)
3. **Debt levels** - Can they service it?
4. **PP&E investment** - Are they investing in growth?

### For Side Hustles:
1. **Track your own financials** like a company
2. **Understand cash vs profit** (income ≠ cash)
3. **Know your assets and liabilities**
4. **Calculate your own "book value"**
